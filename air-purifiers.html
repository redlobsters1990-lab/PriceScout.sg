How to Choose an Air Purifier

1. Calculate Your Room Size

Measure length × width × ceiling height (usually 2.5m in Singapore). For effective air cleaning, you want 4-5 air changes per hour.

  • HDB Bedroom (12m²): Needs 150-250 CADR
  • Master Bedroom (18m²): Needs 200-350 CADR
  • Living Room (30m²): Needs 350-500 CADR

2. Look for True HEPA

Only True HEPA filters remove 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns or larger. This includes PM2.5 from haze. Avoid "HEPA-type" or "HEPA-like" filters.

3. Check Filter Costs

A S$200 purifier with S$100/year filter costs becomes expensive. Xiaomi and Levoit filters cost S$30-50 and last 6-12 months.

4. Consider Noise Levels

For bedrooms, look for under 35dB on sleep mode. For living areas, under 50dB is acceptable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do air purifiers work for Singapore haze?

Yes, if you choose one with True HEPA filter and sufficient CADR (300+ m³/h for PM2.5). Run on high during haze season, keep windows closed.

Where should I place my air purifier?

Place 1-2 meters from walls, away from curtains. Elevated position works better than floor. Keep 50cm clearance on all sides for airflow.

How often should I replace filters?

HEPA filters: 6-12 months depending on usage and air quality. Carbon filters: 3-6 months. Check the filter indicator or set calendar reminders.

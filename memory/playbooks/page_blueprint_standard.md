# Page Blueprint Standard

PriceScout/DAF comparison pages are not generic blog articles.
They are structured comparison pages designed to:
- rank in Google
- help users compare options quickly
- convert visitors into affiliate clicks or leads

## Real-time information rule
When a run is triggered in chat, real-time information is the source of truth.
Use fresh live search/fetch evidence, current product/source links, and current workflow state.
Do not present hardcoded, seeded, synthetic, or stale Python data as if it were current market or buyer truth.

## Operational angle
Frame Singapore-local resilience content through three lenses:
- Cost Pressure
- Disruption Risk
- Mitigation and Preparedness

When generating analysis or website content, always answer:
- What does this development mean for Singapore?
- Could it increase costs locally?
- Could it disrupt supply or operations?
- What should people do differently as a result?

Avoid generic geopolitical commentary. Focus on practical implications for Singapore readers.

## Persuasion objective
Frame developments in a way that encourages readers to:
- think about resilience and preparedness
- recognize cost pressures early
- consider mitigation strategies
- make more informed decisions

## Required sections
Every comparison page must include:
1. SEO title and headline
2. Quick answer summary
3. Above-the-fold comparison table
4. Top picks section
5. Detailed option breakdown
6. Price comparison
7. Pros and cons matrix
8. Buying guide
9. FAQ
10. Final recommendation

## UX design requirements
Design for fast scanning and fast decision-making.
Use:
- bullet lists
- comparison tables
- verdict boxes
- pros/cons cards
- short paragraphs

Users should be able to reach a decision in under 20 seconds.

## Comparison table rule
Place a structured comparison table above the fold.
Required columns:
- Provider/Product
- Price
- Key Feature
- Best For
- Rating
- CTA

## Localization rule
All content must be localized for Singapore.
Use:
- SGD prices
- Singapore retailers or providers
- Singapore consumer considerations

## Conversion design rule
Include:
- clear CTA areas
- affiliate link placement
- value highlights
- price comparisons

## Product link and visual requirement
Do not use fake, placeholder, guessed, synthetic, or search-only links when direct product/seller links are required for a publish-ready page.
Before publish readiness, the workflow must have:
- real product or seller URLs for the compared options
- supporting visuals/images/photos suitable for customer-facing presentation
- clear source attribution for those links/assets

If real links or visuals are missing, planner/builder outputs must explicitly request:
- source merchant/site name
- canonical product or seller URL
- available image/photo URL or approved local asset
- affiliate tag / subid / ref parameter format if applicable
- deeplink pattern or approved link builder format if applicable
- allowed destination type (product page, seller page, category page)
- disclosure/compliance wording requirements

Comparison-page scope is not limited to Shopee, Lazada, or Amazon. Any credible monetizable source may be used.

Product-count rule:
- more than 5 products is allowed when it improves comparison quality
- do not force the page to stop at 5 if the category benefits from a larger shortlist

## Advisory page fallback rule
If a topic is strategically strong but lacks affiliate links, product links, seller links, or visuals/images needed for a full comparison page, do not fail it automatically and do not park it in a deferred enrichment-only state.
Instead, route it to an evidence-led advisory page format when:
- market need is strong
- Singapore relevance is strong
- practical user value is strong
- but monetization/execution inputs are incomplete

Advisory page requirements:
- explain why the topic matters now for Singapore readers
- answer practical local implications
- do not force a fake comparison if the comparison is not genuinely ready
- present the validated information that is available now
- include a clear 'Contact Us for Product Information' section
- include a lead/contact form block requesting product information follow-up
- avoid pretending a comparison page is complete when links/images are missing

## Customer-facing copy rule
Do not surface internal-style phrases such as:
- professional comparison design
- customer-facing language
- cleaner outbound paths

Rewrite them into concrete customer value, for example:
- compare faster with clearer layouts
- understand which option fits your use case
- open verified seller pages instead of broken or vague links

## Output expectation
Planner/builder outputs should provide:
1. Page wireframe
2. Page section structure
3. Key components
4. Content for each section
5. Conversion elements

If a clear blueprint cannot be produced, reject the opportunity.
